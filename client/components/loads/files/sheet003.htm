Instructions:
 
1.  Tail#:  Select Aircraft from dropdown list.

2.  Fuel Dropdown Box:  Select Fuel plan. 
“Round Trip NO ALT” (Round trip fuel, no alternate airport fuel required, returns to base with 1+05hr fuel remaining at pattern altitude flaps up burn rate of 800lbs/hr).

Pilot will need to let planner know of other fuel plan or custom fuel amount.  Custom amount will need to be selected for multi-stop flights. 

******FUEL PLAN DOES NOT ACCOUNT FOR WINDS.  HEADWIND WILL INCREASE ROUND TRIP FUEL REQUIREMENT*********

3.  Enter Flight#

4.  Departure:  3 letter ID. (a few places have a 4 letter ID, see DistanceNM BA PFR column)

5.  Destination:  3 letter ID. (a few places have a 4 letter ID, see DistanceNM BA PFR column)

6. Place the cursor in the first blue box (Captain), enter weight, then “Tab” through the blue fields and enter values.  The worksheet is protected so that only the blue fields can be edited. 

-Captain: Enter weight
-First Officer:  Enter weight
-Jumpseat: Enter weight
-CASA Box: Enter weight of any equipment carried in this location.
-Enter weights in Zone I, II, III, IV, V, and A.  Limits are all listed in the sheet. 
-A good starting point is to use the Recommended Load numbers that are generated in column E.
-BULK FUEL haul cell must be empty if not carried.  If carried, enter Gallons.  The weight of the tank is included.  

7. The form auto calculates all the required information.  Ensure weight and CG are within the charted blue diagram borders.  Any red text is out of a limit and requires adjustment.

8.  Print and give to Captain to carry on flight. 

9.  Pilot turns in with flight paperwork at end of flight.   

10.  Bering Air stores for 30 days.

NOTES:

All distances calculated to show realistc mileage by:
1. Haversine Great Circle formula,  
2. Rounded up to nearest 1nm, and
3. Added 3nm for terminal area distance.

Some routes have special routing rules to give more accurate actual distance, as follows: 
1. All flights between SVA/GAM/OHC and mainland north of 63N and ANC are routed via shorter of CUMNS or ZONBA. 
2. All flights between SVA/GAM/OHC and mainland south of 63N other than ANC are direct.  Liferaft/vests may be required. 
3. All flights between PHO/LUR and OME are routed via SUGRE. 
4. All flights between NW and SW Alaska are routed via ..TOCOT-POYIT.. for single engine glide distance.  Multi-engine may have shorter routes available while remaining within 50mn of shore, or extended overwater more direct with liferaft/vests onboard. 
5. All flights to DRG from north of DRG route via JIKSA, except LUR, PHO, KVL are direct.  
6. All flights between UNK and airports northwest of 64N/162.5W routed via AHCUJ-THEPS.

***these routes are not required to be flown, rather used to add reasonable mileage for basic calculations***

***this program is for basic planning purposes only, check against other planning tools available***

***WIND NOT ACCOUNTED FOR***

***For long flights, especially one way or high altitude, ForeFlight Performance Pro (BE20) will be more accurate.  For other aircraft, the basic Foreflight will still be more accurate when wind and cruise altitude are included***

*** Verify distances, as they were gathered using AI.  Email benr@beringair.com for corrections, additions, or information***

***PIC Plan and fly route as necessary***

Standard Performance Criteria
Standard performance may be used if ALL the following conditions are met.  The speeds were derived from AFM performance data and have an error factor of +/- 3kts.  The TO and LAND distances provided include all of the criteria below at their most restrictive level.     

1. Engine Intake – OFF
2. OAT 15℃ or less
3. Pressure altitude 1000’ or less
4. No tailwind
5. Dry runway
6. TO/LAND runway > TO/LAND DIST available.
7. Takeoff runway gradient .5% to -1%
8. Landing runway gradient 2% to -1%
